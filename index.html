About Me

I'm a game designer/programmer, technical writer, composer, and audio designer who is deeply fascinated by the performant and emergent experiences created by dynamic, deep, demanding gameplay systems.

I've worked for 8 years in a variety of different capacities in online learning, technical writing, and instructional design. I currently work on a variety of safety training, documentation, and instructional design.

In 2012, I started taking computer music composition seriously, after briefly entertaining the idea of forming a band with friends a year or two prior. My first published release was the original soundtrack for the free Android game Laser Ninja. I am primarily a pianist and a vocalist, but have also taken lessons in violin and guitar.

In 2013, I took my first game development course in college and have been working on a variety of independent game projects since then, several of which have been tragically lost. I often found myself filling audio and music roles on teams of mostly graphic designers and programmers, but eventually found that the design, programming, and math behind the game-play is my primary interest

In 2019, I released my first original game, Space Drifter. All assets are original and it was built with vanilla JavaScript, HTML5, and CSS.
